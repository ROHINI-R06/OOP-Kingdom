# ⚔️ Chapter 03: The Watchers

## The Golden Days

The Velfire trade changed everything.

What had once been a struggling settlement on the edge of survival became a bustling hub of commerce. Carts rolled in from distant cities — not just the trader's single cart, but entire caravans. Merchants came to see the legendary purple stone for themselves. Craftsmen came to learn its properties. Scholars came to study it. The curious, the ambitious, the desperate — all found their way to the valley.

The Market, once a handful of stalls, expanded into a proper square. The Storehouse bulged with grain and resources. The Ore Processor worked day and night. The Mine's tunnels stretched deeper beneath the ridge.

The Library finally got its scrolls.

The scholar wept when the first shipment of books arrived — histories, maps, treaties, poems. She filled the shelves with trembling hands. The Library was no longer half-baked. It was alive.

For the first time since the exile, the OOP Kingdom felt like a real city.

## The Shadow

But prosperity has a price.

With more people came more problems. A merchant reported goods missing from his stall. A traveler claimed his purse was stolen while he slept at the inn. A farmer's tools vanished overnight. Fights broke out in the Market square. Suspicion festered between newcomers and original settlers.

The Warrior — now grey-haired and slower on her feet — sat in the City Hall and listened to the complaints pile up.

"We have no one to oversee this," she said. "No patrols. No watch. No authority to investigate or judge."

The council had no answer. There was no building for it. No role for it. The city had grown faster than its systems could handle.

## The Solution

It was the blacksmith who spoke first — the same blacksmith who had handed the stranger the first piece of velfire.

"We need eyes everywhere. Small posts scattered through the city. Every district. Every lane. A guard at every post who knows the people, knows the streets, knows when something is wrong."

"A **Guard Post** in every neighborhood," the trader nodded. "And one central **Guard Station** to coordinate them all."

"Where do the criminals go once caught?" asked the farmer.

"The **Prison**," said the blacksmith.

"And who decides their fate?" asked the scholar.

The Warrior smiled. "The **Court House**. With a judge who hears both sides before passing sentence."

"What about the gates?" called a voice from the back. "If we don't know who enters and leaves, criminals will just walk out."

"A **Gatehouse**," the Warrior agreed. "To log every visitor, every departure."

"And someone to spread the word," the Town Crier added. "When the court passes a verdict, when a guard post needs help, when the city needs to know something — someone must announce it. That's my role."

The Warrior looked around the room. Six buildings. Six roles. A complete system of law and order.

"Then let it be done," she said. "Week Three of the OOP Kingdom begins. Build us a system of justice — from the smallest patrol to the highest court."

*And so the call went out once more. The kingdom was no longer just surviving — it was thriving. But a thriving city without order is a city waiting to fall. The Watchers would rise to keep it standing.*
